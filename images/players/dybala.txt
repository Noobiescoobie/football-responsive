Commonly referred to as "La Joya" ("The Jewel"),[4] or "U Picciriddu" ("The Kid"; mostly by Palermitans),[5] Dybala began his club career in 2011 with Instituto de Córdoba; he joined Palermo in 2012, at age 18, in a transfer worth a reported €8 million, where he established himself as one of the world's best young players and won a Serie B title. In 2015, Dybala signed for Juventus for an initial €32 million fee, and has won five league titles
